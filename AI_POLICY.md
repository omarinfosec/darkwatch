# AI Usage Policy

> **Attribution.** This policy is adapted from the [Ghostty AI Usage Policy](https://github.com/ghostty-org/ghostty/blob/main/AI_POLICY.md). DarkWatch adopts the same intent and rules for outside contributions. I thank the Ghostty maintainers for publishing a clear, practical policy.

DarkWatch has strict rules for AI usage by **outside contributors**:

- **All AI usage in any form must be disclosed.** You must state the tool you used (e.g. Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot) along with the extent that the work was AI-assisted.
- **The human-in-the-loop must fully understand all code.** If you can't explain what your changes do and how they interact with the greater system without the aid of AI tools, do not contribute to this project.
- **Issues and discussions can use AI assistance but must have a full human-in-the-loop.** This means that any content generated with AI must have been reviewed *and edited* by a human before submission. AI is very good at being overly verbose and including noise that distracts from the main point. Humans must do their research and trim this down.
- **Bad AI drivers will be blocked.** People who produce bad contributions that are clearly unverified AI output may be blocked from future contributions. I want to help people learn and grow, but contributions must be understood, tested, and owned by the contributor — not dumped on me to validate.

These rules apply only to **outside contributions** to DarkWatch. I am exempt from these rules as the maintainer and may use AI tools at my discretion.

**My tooling.** I use **Claude** and **Cursor** when writing and maintaining this repository. DarkWatch has no LLM/API integration at runtime. All shipped changes remain human-reviewed and tested by me.

## There is a Human Here

Please remember that DarkWatch is maintained by one person.

Every discussion, issue, and pull request is read and reviewed by me (and sometimes machines, too). It is a boundary point at which people interact with the work. It is rude and disrespectful to approach this boundary with low-effort, unqualified work, since it puts the burden of validation on me.

In a perfect world, AI would produce high-quality, accurate work every time. But today, that reality depends on the driver of the AI. And today, most drivers of AI are just not good enough. So, until either people get better, the AI gets better, or both, I have to have strict rules to protect my time and the quality of this project.

## AI is Welcome Here

DarkWatch is written with AI assistance, and I embrace AI tools as a productive part of my workflow. As a project, I welcome AI as a tool!

**My reason for the strict AI policy is not due to an anti-AI stance**, but instead due to the number of highly unqualified people using AI. It's the people, not the tools, that are the problem.

I include this section to be transparent about my use of AI for people who may disagree with it, and to address the misconception that this policy is anti-AI in nature.
